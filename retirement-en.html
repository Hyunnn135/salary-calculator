What is Severance Pay?

Severance pay is a legally mandated payment that employers must provide to workers upon leaving the company after a certain period of service. Under Article 34 of the Labor Standards Act, any worker who has worked at least 15 hours per week and has been continuously employed for 1 year or more is entitled to severance pay, regardless of the reason for leaving.

In principle, severance pay must be paid within 14 days after leaving the company. If this is violated, the employer must pay additional late interest (20% per year).

How to Calculate Severance Pay

Severance Pay = Daily Average Wage x 30 days x (Service Days / 365)

Daily Average Wage = Total wages received during the 3 months before leaving / Total number of days in that period (approx. 91 days)

The key factor is the average wage. It is calculated by adding up the base salary, various allowances, and bonuses (prorated monthly) from the 3 months immediately before leaving, then dividing by the number of days in that period.

If your monthly average salary is 3 million KRW and you worked for 5 years, your severance pay is approximately 3 million KRW x 5 = approx. 15 million KRW (1 year of work = approximately 1 month's salary accumulated as severance)

What is Retirement Income Tax?

Severance pay is subject to Retirement Income Tax. Unlike regular earned income tax, since it is income accumulated over a long period, deduction benefits based on Years of Service are significant.

  • The longer your Years of Service, the larger the retirement income deduction, resulting in a lower effective tax rate
  • 5 years of service: 5 million KRW deduction / 10 years: 15 million KRW deduction / 20 years: 40 million KRW deduction
  • If the severance pay is small or the Years of Service is short, there may be almost no tax

The Retirement Income Tax in this calculator is an estimate. Please confirm the exact tax amount with your employer or through the National Tax Service (Hometax) when you leave your job.

Severance Pay vs Retirement Pension (IRP)

Recently, many companies operate a retirement pension system instead of lump-sum severance pay. Retirement pensions are divided into DB (Defined Benefit) and DC (Defined Contribution) types.

  • DB type: The benefit amount at retirement is predetermined. The calculation method is the same as regular severance pay
  • DC type: The amount the company contributes each year is fixed. The payout varies depending on investment performance
  • Mandatory IRP transfer: If severance pay exceeds 3 million KRW, it must be received through an IRP (Individual Retirement Pension) account

FAQ

Q. When can I receive severance pay after leaving?
Under the Labor Standards Act, it must be paid within 14 days of the date of leaving. In case of delay, late interest of 20% per year is incurred, and you can report it to the Labor Office.
Q. Can contract workers or part-timers receive severance pay?
Regardless of employment type, if you have worked continuously for 1 year or more and averaged at least 15 hours per week, you are entitled to severance pay.
Q. Do I have to receive severance pay through an IRP account?
If severance pay exceeds 3 million KRW, it is mandatory to receive it through an IRP (Individual Retirement Pension) account. When received via IRP, tax payment is deferred, and if received as a pension after age 55, the tax rate is reduced.

FAQ (Continued)

Q. Is severance pay taxed?
Retirement Income Tax is applied. The longer your service period, the larger the deduction amount, resulting in a lower effective tax rate. Workers with 5 years or less of service often pay almost no tax.
Q. Are bonuses included in the severance pay calculation?
The basis is wages paid during the 3 months before leaving. Regularly paid monthly bonuses are included, but special bonuses paid as a one-time payment just before leaving may not be included.
Q. What should I do if the company does not pay severance?
You can file a complaint with the Ministry of Employment and Labor customer center (1350) or the nearest Regional Employment and Labor Office. Non-payment of severance is a criminal offense punishable by up to 3 years in prison or a fine of up to 20 million KRW.

Detailed Guide to the Severance Pay System

Severance pay is a financial compensation received when a worker leaves after working for a certain period, guaranteed under the Employee Retirement Benefit Security Act. Understanding severance pay accurately can be an important factor when planning when to leave a job or deciding on a career change.

Severance Pay Eligibility

To receive severance pay, you must have continuous employment of 1 year or more, and average at least 15 scheduled working hours per week over a 4-week period. Regardless of employment type — full-time, contract, or part-time — you are eligible for severance pay if you meet these two conditions. The employer must pay severance within 14 days of the worker's last day.

How to Calculate Severance Pay

Severance Pay = (Daily Average Wage × 30 days) × (Total Service Days ÷ 365). The Daily Average Wage is calculated by dividing the total wages paid during the 3 months before leaving by the total number of days in that period. Items included in the average wage are base salary, fixed allowances, and regularly paid bonuses. Expense reimbursements (actual transportation costs, business trip expenses, etc.), congratulatory/condolence payments, and one-time bonuses are not included.

Interim Severance Settlement

Interim severance settlement is prohibited in principle, but it is allowed if there are legally specified reasons. Major reasons include home purchase by a homeless person, cases where the worker or a dependent needs medical treatment for 6 months or more, bankruptcy declaration or personal rehabilitation proceedings within the past 5 years, and application of the salary peak system.

Severance Pay Tax and IRP

Severance pay is subject to Retirement Income Tax. Deductions based on Years of Service and differential taxation based on converted salary are applied, so the longer the Years of Service, the lower the tax burden. If severance pay is transferred to an IRP (Individual Retirement Pension) account, Retirement Income Tax payment can be deferred until pension withdrawal, and when received as a pension, only 60-70% of the Retirement Income Tax is charged, providing a tax-saving benefit.

What to Do If Severance Pay Is Not Paid

If the employer does not pay severance within 14 days after the employee leaves, it constitutes a violation of the Labor Standards Act. First, request payment from the company in writing, and if the issue is not resolved, you can consult with the Ministry of Employment and Labor (1350) or file a complaint with the regional Employment and Labor Office. Late interest on unpaid severance is 20% per year, and the statute of limitations for severance claims is 3 years, so it is important to take action as soon as possible. If the company goes bankrupt and you cannot receive severance pay, you can use the Korea Workers' Compensation & Welfare Service's unpaid wage payment system, and you can receive support for a significant portion of the unpaid wages.
