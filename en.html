What is Take-Home Pay?

Take-home pay is the amount you actually receive after deducting National Pension, Health Insurance, Employment Insurance, and income taxes from your gross salary.

Typically, take-home pay in Korea is about 75–85% of your gross annual salary.

FAQ

What's the take-home for a ₩50M salary?
For a single person, approximately ₩3.58M per month. It varies depending on dependents and non-taxable amounts.
What is the non-taxable amount?
It includes meal allowances (up to ₩200,000/month) and other tax-exempt items shown on your payslip. If unsure, set it to 0.
Why does my result differ from my actual pay?
This is an estimate based on the simplified tax table. Actual amounts may vary due to year-end tax settlement and company policies.
