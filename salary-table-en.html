FAQ

What standards are used to calculate the take-home pay in the table?
Calculated based on 1 dependent (self) with no tax-free allowances. More dependents or tax-free items will result in higher take-home pay.
Why is the amount different from my actual pay stub?
Differences arise from tax-free items (meal/transport allowances), number of dependents, and additional deductions.
Can take-home pay differ for the same annual salary?
Yes. Depending on the number of dependents, tax-free amounts, and additional deductions, take-home pay can differ even for the same annual salary.
How much tax is there for annual salaries over 100M KRW?
For an annual salary of 100M KRW, monthly take-home pay is approximately 6,400,000 KRW, which is about 77% of the gross monthly salary.

Annual Take-Home Pay Analysis by Salary Range

As salary increases, the take-home ratio decreases. This is due to Korea's progressive income tax structure and increasing social insurance premiums. The analysis below helps predict actual income changes when receiving a raise or changing jobs.

24M~30M KRW Range

This range has the highest take-home ratio with deductions of about 12-14%. The 6% income tax bracket applies, keeping tax burden relatively low. Social insurance premiums are also proportional to income. The 2026 minimum wage-based annual salary (approx. 25,880,000 KRW) falls within this range.

35M~50M KRW Range

This is the boundary where the income tax rate transitions from the 6% bracket to the 15% bracket. The deduction rate is about 15-18%, and you start to feel the income tax burden from this range. For an annual salary of 40M KRW, monthly take-home pay is approximately 2,840,000 KRW, which is about 85% of the pre-tax monthly salary (approx. 3,330,000 KRW).

60M~80M KRW Range

Deductions reach 18-20%, and this is where Health Insurance premium increases become truly felt. The 24% income tax bracket applies, making tax burden significant. The gap between salary raises and actual take-home pay increases starts to widen in this range.

100M KRW and Above

Income tax rates of 35% or higher apply, and the deduction rate rises to 22-28%. For an annual salary of 100M KRW, monthly take-home pay is approximately 6,520,000 KRW, about 78% of the pre-tax monthly salary (approx. 8,330,000 KRW). At 200M KRW, the deduction rate reaches about 28%, further lowering the take-home ratio.

Tax Reduction Strategies Using Tax-Free Items

The higher your salary, the greater the tax-saving effect of tax-free items. By maximizing tax-free allowances such as meal allowance (200,000 KRW/month), personal vehicle subsidy (200,000 KRW/month), and childcare allowance (200,000 KRW/month), you can lower your taxable income and reduce income tax. When negotiating your salary, it is worth checking with your employer whether tax-free items can be structured separately.

Take-Home Changes When Salary Increases

When your salary increases, your take-home pay also increases, but not by as much as the raise itself. For example, even if your salary increases 25% from 40M KRW to 50M KRW, take-home pay only increases by about 20-22%. This is because the progressive income tax bracket rises and social insurance premiums proportional to income also increase. When changing jobs or negotiating salary, comparing based on take-home pay rather than just pre-tax salary will help you accurately assess the actual change in your standard of living.
