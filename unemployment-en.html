Eligibility Requirements for Unemployment Benefits

You must meet all of the following conditions to receive unemployment benefits.

Employment Insurance Period

You must have been enrolled in employment insurance for at least 180 days within the 18 months before your last day of work.

Involuntary resignation

In principle, you cannot receive unemployment benefits if you quit voluntarily.

Willingness to Re-employ

You must be actively seeking re-employment. You need to periodically apply for unemployment certification.

Application Filing

You must apply within 12 months after leaving your job. If the period expires, your eligibility will be forfeited.

Cases where voluntary resignation qualifies: Unpaid wages, workplace harassment, relocation of workplace to an unreachable distance, pregnancy/childbirth/childcare, health deterioration, and other justifiable reasons for leaving may make you eligible.

How to Calculate Benefit Amounts

Daily unemployment benefit (job-seeking allowance) = 60% of pre-departure average wage

  • Daily upper limit: 68,100 KRW (2026 standard)
  • Daily lower limit: 66,048 KRW (based on 2026 minimum wage)

Regardless of how high or low your salary is, benefits are paid within the upper and lower limits above.

Example: Monthly salary 3 million KRW, 3-5 years insured (under 50) → daily 66,048 KRW × 180 days = approx. 11.89 million KRW

FAQ

What are the conditions to receive unemployment benefits?
You must have at least 180 days of employment insurance coverage within the 18 months before leaving, and the departure must be involuntary (recommended resignation, contract expiration, etc.).
Can I receive unemployment benefits even if I resigned voluntarily?
In principle, voluntary resignation does not qualify. However, if there are unavoidable circumstances such as workplace harassment, unpaid wages, changes in working conditions, or inability to commute, you may exceptionally qualify for benefits.
Can I do part-time work while receiving benefits?
Yes, but you must report it to the Employment Center. If you work or earn income without reporting, it will be treated as fraudulent receipt of benefits.
What happens to health insurance premiums while receiving benefits?
After leaving your job, you will be converted to a regional subscriber and must pay health insurance premiums. However, if you meet the dependent registration requirements, you can be included in a family member's workplace insurance.

Detailed Guide to Unemployment Benefits Application and Eligibility

Unemployment benefits are payments provided to workers enrolled in employment insurance who involuntarily leave their jobs, to support financial stability during the period of job-seeking activities. Understanding the exact eligibility requirements and application process helps you respond quickly to job loss and reduce financial burden.

Eligibility Requirements

Two main conditions must be met to receive unemployment benefits. First, the separation must be involuntary. This includes recommended resignation, contract expiration, workplace relocation, and violation of working conditions. Second, you must have at least 180 total days of insured employment within the 18 months before leaving. Even if you resigned voluntarily, you may qualify if there are recognized reasons such as unpaid wages, wages below minimum wage, workplace harassment or sexual harassment, or workplace relocation requiring a one-way commute of 1.5 hours or more.

Benefit Amount and Payment Period

The daily benefit amount is 60% of the average wage for the 3 months before leaving, with a daily upper limit of 66,000 KRW and a lower limit of 80% of the minimum wage. Based on the 2026 minimum wage (10,320 KRW), the lower limit is 66,048 KRW per day (based on 8 hours), which is nearly the same as the upper limit. The prescribed benefit days vary from 120 to a maximum of 270 days depending on age and employment insurance period. Generally, the older you are and the longer your insurance period, the longer you can receive benefits.

Application Process

The unemployment benefits application follows these steps: (1) Register on WorkNet (work.go.kr): Register for job seeking online as soon as possible after leaving. (2) Apply for eligibility certification: Visit the Employment Center in your jurisdiction and submit the eligibility certification application. (3) Complete eligibility training: Complete online or in-person training. (4) Job-seeking activities and unemployment certification: Provide proof of at least 2 re-employment activities every 4 weeks to receive unemployment certification. (5) Receive benefits: Payment is deposited into the designated account approximately 2 weeks after the unemployment certification date.

Important Notes While Receiving Benefits

Even while receiving unemployment benefits, you must follow several important rules. You must engage in job-seeking activities at least twice every 4 weeks, including job applications, interview participation, and vocational training. Simply browsing job postings alone does not qualify. Additionally, if you get a job or earn income while receiving benefits, you must report it, and in cases of fraudulent receipt, you may be ordered to return up to 5 times the amount received. During the benefit period, National Pension is converted to voluntary enrollment, and health insurance must be maintained by registering as a dependent or converting to a regional subscriber. For detailed consultation on unemployment benefits, contact the Employment Insurance website (ei.go.kr) or the Employment Center (1350).
