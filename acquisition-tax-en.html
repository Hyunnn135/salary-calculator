Acquisition Tax Key Points

Filing & Payment Deadline: You must file and pay at the local district office within 60 days from the property balance payment date. Late filing incurs penalty taxes.

Regulated Area Rates: Tax rates for multi-home owners vary significantly based on whether the property is in a regulated area. 2 houses in a regulated area incur 8%, and 3+ houses incur 12%.

Temporary 2-House Exception: If you temporarily own 2 houses due to relocation, the standard 1-house rate applies if you sell the previous house within 3 years.

FAQ

When do I need to pay acquisition tax?
You must file and pay within 60 days from whichever comes first: the balance payment date or registration date. Late filing incurs a 20% penalty tax.
What is a Regulated Area?
A regulated area is a zone designated by the government to control overheating in the real estate market. Acquiring multiple houses in a regulated area triggers heavy tax rates, significantly increasing the tax burden.
Is there a first-time homebuyer tax reduction?
Yes. First-time homebuyers can receive an acquisition tax reduction of up to 2,000,000 KRW. There are specific conditions regarding income and property price that need to be verified.
Is there acquisition tax on pre-sale rights?
Yes. Acquisition tax is not paid at the time of the pre-sale rights transaction itself, but when you complete the final payment and receive ownership transfer. Pre-sale rights are also counted in the number of houses calculation.

Tax Rates & Reduction Benefits

Acquisition tax is a local tax levied when acquiring assets such as real estate or vehicles. It is one of the largest ancillary costs when purchasing a house, potentially ranging from hundreds of thousands to tens of millions of KRW depending on the purchase price, making accurate pre-calculation essential.

Housing Acquisition Tax Rates

When purchasing a house, the acquisition tax rate varies by purchase price. Properties at 600M KRW or below are taxed at 1%, 600M-900M KRW at 1-3% (the rate increases progressively with price), and above 900M KRW at 3%. For multi-home owners, heavy taxation applies: 2 houses in a regulated area are taxed at 8%, and 3+ houses at 12%. When a corporation acquires a house, a flat 12% rate applies.

Acquisition Tax Reduction Benefits

For first-time homebuyers, an acquisition tax reduction of up to 2,000,000 KRW is available for houses with a transaction price of 1.2 billion KRW or less. Newlywed couples who purchase a house within 5 years of marriage registration can receive reductions for houses priced at 400M KRW or less in the Seoul metropolitan area, or 300M KRW or less outside the metropolitan area. There are also various reduction provisions for rural houses, housing for disabled persons, etc., so it is advisable to check eligibility carefully.

Payment Deadline and Additional Taxes

Acquisition tax must be filed and paid within 60 days from the acquisition date (whichever comes first: balance payment date or registration date). Late filing incurs penalty taxes, so caution is needed. In addition to acquisition tax, Rural Special Tax (10% of acquisition tax) and Local Education Tax (10-20% of acquisition tax) are also levied, so the actual burden is higher than the acquisition tax alone. Filing and payment can be conveniently processed online at Wetax (wetax.go.kr).

Acquisition Tax for Non-Housing Real Estate and Vehicles

Non-housing real estate (commercial buildings, officetels, land, etc.) is generally subject to a 4% acquisition tax rate. Note that officetels are taxed at 4% if they are classified as office facilities in the building registry, even if used for residential purposes. For vehicles, non-commercial passenger cars are taxed at 7%, while commercial vehicles, compact cars, and trucks are taxed at 4%. Disabled persons and national merit recipients are exempt from vehicle acquisition tax, and compact cars (engine displacement 1,000cc or less) are exempt from acquisition tax (for purchase prices of 40M KRW or less). When acquiring real estate, it is wise to factor in additional costs such as legal fees and brokerage commissions in addition to acquisition tax when calculating the total cost.
