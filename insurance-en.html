What is Social Insurance?

The 4 major insurance programs are 4 mandatory public insurance systems in Korea. Workers and employers share the premium costs.

  • National Pension — Secures retirement income. Pension payments begin after age 60
  • Health Insurance — Reduces hospital costs. Employees pay only a portion of actual medical expenses
  • Long-term Care — Provides care services for elderly or disabled individuals with limited mobility
  • Employment Insurance — Provides unemployment benefits, parental leave pay, and other support

FAQ

Do part-time workers need to enroll in social insurance?
If the prescribed weekly working hours are 15 or more, enrollment in all 4 insurance programs is mandatory. If under 15 hours, National Pension and Employment Insurance may be excluded.
Why doesn't my pension premium increase even when my salary goes up?
National Pension has a monthly standard income cap (6.17 million KRW). Premiums are not charged on salary exceeding this cap.
How do freelancers pay social insurance?
Freelancers (self-employed) enroll as individual subscribers for National Pension and Health Insurance, paying the full premium themselves.

2026 Social Insurance Guide

The 4 major insurance programs — National Pension, Health Insurance, Long-term Care, and Employment Insurance — are jointly funded by workers and employers. Premiums are automatically deducted and vary based on income. Understanding your exact premium amounts can greatly help with salary management.

National Pension (Total 9%, worker 4.5%)

National Pension is a social insurance program for retirement income security. The 2026 standard monthly income cap is 6.37 million KRW, and the floor is 390,000 KRW. Even if income exceeds the cap, premiums are calculated based on 6.37 million KRW only. Workers aged 18 to under 60 are eligible for enrollment, and pension benefits can be received from age 65. Premiums paid fund old-age pensions, disability pensions, and survivor pensions.

Health Insurance (Total 7.19%, worker 3.595%)

Health Insurance reduces the medical cost burden for illness or injury. The 2026 rate is 7.19%, slightly increased from the previous year. Premiums are proportional to income, and unlike National Pension, there is no income cap. Dependents of workplace subscribers (spouse, parents, etc.) can receive health insurance benefits without paying additional premiums.

Long-term Care (13.14% of Health Insurance Premium)

Long-term Care Insurance provides care services for individuals aged 65 or older, or those with geriatric diseases. Premiums are linked to Health Insurance and set at 13.14% of Health Insurance premiums in 2026. If you are a Health Insurance subscriber, premiums are automatically charged without a separate enrollment process.

Employment Insurance (Total 1.8%, worker 0.9%)

Employment Insurance funds unemployment benefits, parental leave pay, and vocational training. The employee contribution is 0.9%, while employers pay an additional 0.9% to 1.5% depending on company size. Workers with under 15 hours per week, and workers newly hired after age 65, may be excluded from Employment Insurance. Freelancers (subject to 3.3% withholding) must separately enroll in National Pension and Health Insurance as individual subscribers rather than workplace subscribers.

Useful Tips About Social Insurance

Social insurance premiums are settled annually through total compensation reporting. If actual income differs from reported income, premium differences may be additionally charged or refunded. For Health Insurance, dependent qualification requirements are being gradually tightened. If a dependent's income or assets exceed certain thresholds, they may be reclassified as individual subscribers, so caution is needed. You can check your social insurance enrollment status and premium details at the Social Insurance Information Center (4insure.or.kr).
